Grapes are a truly fascinating fruit, enjoyed by cultures around the globe for thousands of years. Whether fresh, dried as raisins, or fermented into wine, these juicy berries are a versatile and delicious treat, growing in beautiful clusters on vines.

Types of Grapes

  • Table Grapes: These are varieties grown to be eaten fresh, known for their crisp texture and sweet flavor. Popular examples include Thompson Seedless, Concord, and Crimson Seedless.
  • Wine Grapes: With hundreds of unique varieties, wine grapes are cultivated specifically for winemaking. Famous types include Chardonnay, Merlot, Pinot Noir, and Cabernet Sauvignon, each contributing distinct characteristics to wines.
  • Raisin Grapes: Often seedless varieties like Thompson Seedless are carefully dried under the sun or by mechanical means to produce plump, sweet raisins, a popular snack and baking ingredient.

Health Benefits

Beyond their delicious taste, grapes are packed with essential nutrients! They are a good source of Vitamin C and K, and rich in powerful antioxidants, particularly resveratrol, which has been linked to various health benefits, including supporting heart health and combating oxidative stress.
